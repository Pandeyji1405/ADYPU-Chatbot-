Core Responsibilities
* Event Leadership: Conceptualizing and executing university-wide fests (#TECH, Impetus, Ablaze).
* Student Welfare: Addressing grievances related to campus life, hostels, and academic facilities.
* Institutional Governance: Active participation in committees like Anti-Ragging, Gender Sensitization, and Disciplinary Committees.
* Feedback Loop: Providing student insights on the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 implementation and curriculum relevance.
* Social Responsibility: Organizing blood donation camps, "Joy of Giving" weeks, and NSS initiatives.

Election & Selection Process
Council members are selected annually through a rigorous process that balances democratic elections with merit-based selection.
1. Eligibility: Usually requires a minimum CGPA (often 7.0 or above) and a clean disciplinary record.
2. Campaigning: Candidates for key posts engage in a dedicated week of campus campaigning and manifestos.
3. Voting/Selection: A combination of student voting and faculty interviews determines the final office bearers.
4. Investiture Ceremony: The official induction where members take their oath, typically held in August or September.

NATIONAL STUDENT SERVICE (NSS)
The National Service Scheme (NSS) at Ajeenkya DY Patil University (ADYPU) is a community-focused program aimed at developing social responsibility and leadership in students.
Core Components
* Regular Activities (135 hours/year): Includes tree plantation, blood donation camps, cleanliness drives (e.g., Gandhi Jayanti), and seeds sowing.
* Special Camps: Annual seven-day camps where 50% of volunteers live in groups and work on national development issues like rural reconstruction and literacy.
* Village Adoption: Long-term engagement with a selected nearby village to address specific problems like health, sanitation, and education.
* National Initiatives: Participation in the Red Ribbon Club (AIDS awareness) and Disaster Management Training workshops.
Recent Projects
* Rejuvenation of the Warje Nalla.
* School wall painting at Solu village.
* Regular health and awareness programs in adopted rural areas.
Leadership
* Director: Shital Dilip Solanki (Assistant Professor).
* Focus: Integrating rural community needs with student education to foster national integration and civic sense.
How to Volunteer
Students can engage in orientation sessions featuring field visits and lectures to understand the community's needs before committing to specific rural or urban projects.

DOCTORAL PROGRAMS
Ajeenkya DY Patil University (ADYPU) offers Ph.D. programs across various disciplines, focusing on industry-driven research and multidisciplinary innovation.
Thrust Areas (Specializations)
* School of Engineering: Biomedical Engineering, Biotechnology, Physics, Mathematics, Mechanical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, AI, Robotics, Computer Science, and Bioinformatics.
* School of Management: Entrepreneurship & Innovation, Strategic Management, HR, Organizational Behaviour, Marketing, and Finance.
* School of Law: Law & Governance, Criminal Law, Constitutional Law, Labor/Industrial Laws, Human Rights, Administrative Law, and IPR.
* School of Liberal Arts: Economics and Sociology.
* School of Design: Industrial Design.
Duration & Key Features
* Duration: Minimum 3 years (including course work) to a maximum of 6 years.
* Relaxations: Women and Persons with Disability (>40%) may receive a relaxation of up to 2 years and maternity/child care leave of up to 240 days.
* Mentorship: Guided by internationally acclaimed post-doctoral guides and industry-focused supervisors.
* Scholarships: Available for meritorious research scholars based on potential and academic excellence.
Admission Process
1. Notification: Usually issued with a last date for application of March 31st (check for specific session updates).
2. Eligibility: Requires a Master's degree in the relevant field with a minimum percentage (typically 55%).
3. Entrance Test (PET): Evaluates research aptitude and subject-specific knowledge.
4. Personal Interview: Shortlisted candidates must present their research interest and aptitude to a committee of supervisors.
Supervision
The university has over 40 doctoral supervisors specializing in niche areas like Graph Theory, Nano-materials, Cyber Security, Consumer Behaviour, and Environmental Economics.
To help you prepare, here is the entrance test structure and syllabus overview for the Ph.D. Entrance Test (PET) at Ajeenkya DY Patil University:
Examination Structure
The PET typically consists of two papers, usually conducted on the same day:
1. Paper I: Research Methodology (50%) - Common for all candidates. It tests your aptitude for academic research.
2. Paper II: Subject-Specific (50%) - Based on your chosen school (Engineering, Management, Law, etc.).

Syllabus Overview
1. Paper I: Research Methodology (Common)
* Foundations of Research: Meaning, objectives, and types of research.
* Research Design: Identifying problems, formulating hypotheses, and sampling techniques.
* Data Collection & Analysis: Quantitative and qualitative methods, scaling techniques, and hypothesis testing (t-test, z-test, ANOVA).
* Ethical Practices: Plagiarism, research ethics, and citation styles (APA, MLA, etc.).
* Information Technology: Use of search engines, digital libraries, and basic statistical software.
2. Paper II: Subject-Specific (School-Wise)
* School of Engineering: * Core: Applied Mathematics and Calculus.
o Specialized: Based on your branch (e.g., AI algorithms for CSE; Thermodynamics for Mechanical; Molecular Biology for Biotech).
* School of Management:
o Core: Managerial Economics, Organizational Behavior, and Strategic Management.
o Specialized: Financial Management, Marketing Theory, or HR Analytics.
* School of Law:
o Core: Jurisprudence and Constitutional Law.
o Specialized: Research methods in Law, Intellectual Property Rights, or Administrative Law.
* School of Liberal Arts:
o Core: Critical Thinking and Social Theory.
o Specialized: Advanced topics in Economics, Sociology, or English Literature.

Personal Interview & Research Aptitude
Candidates who clear the written test are invited for an interview where they must:
* Present a Research Proposal (Statement of Purpose).
* Demonstrate their depth of knowledge in the proposed thrust area.
* Discuss the feasibility and industry relevance of their research idea.
Next Steps
Since the last date for the current cycle is March 31st, you should:
1. Identify a potential Supervisor from the ADYPU list whose thrust area matches your interest.
2. Prepare a 500-1000 word Research Abstract.
