Bachelor of Science – Physics

Program Overview

The B.Sc. in Physics program at ADYPU is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of the fundamental principles of physics, while also integrating modern scientific advancements through diverse specializations. The curriculum blends theoretical knowledge with experimental skills, preparing students to explore the universe—from subatomic particles to cosmic structures—and harness the principles of nature to develop innovative solutions in science and technology.

Duration: 4 Years (8 Semesters)

Eligibility:

10+2 or equivalent examination with Physics and Mathematics as core subjects from a recognized board, with a minimum aggregate as specified by the university.

Program Objectives

To build a strong foundation in classical and modern physics concepts.

To develop analytical, mathematical, and computational skills essential for scientific research and problem-solving.

To expose students to interdisciplinary areas including nanotechnology, astrophysics, and artificial intelligence.

To encourage innovation through hands-on laboratory work, research projects, and seminars.

To prepare graduates for advanced study or professional careers in academia, industry, or research.

How You Will Be Taught

The teaching methodology combines:

Lectures & Seminars: Delivered by experienced faculty and guest experts.

Laboratory Sessions: Intensive practical work to reinforce theoretical concepts.

Projects & Research Work: Two dedicated research-based projects in the final year.

Workshops & Industry Exposure: Interactions with scientists and participation in co-curricular activities.

Digital Learning Tools: Utilization of simulations, coding, and data analysis platforms.

Interdisciplinary Courses: Integration with fields like material science, AI, and quantum computing.

Specializations

B.Sc. Physics with Specialization in Quantum Physics

This forward-thinking specialization merges physical sciences with computational intelligence. Students gain expertise in machine learning, big data processing, and quantum computing while applying these tools to physics-based problems—ranging from climate modeling to quantum simulations. Graduates are equipped to lead in fields where data meets discovery, across industries, research labs, and policy organizations.

B.Sc. Physics with Specialization in Nano Materials

This specialization focuses on the manipulation of matter at the nanoscale, combining physics with chemistry, engineering, and material science. Students gain hands-on experience in nanofabrication, quantum mechanics, and electron microscopy. Graduates are equipped to work on innovative technologies such as drug delivery systems, nanosensors, and energy-efficient materials—preparing them for roles in R&D labs, advanced manufacturing, and the nanotech industry.

B.Sc. Physics with Specialization in Astrophysics

Dive into the mysteries of the cosmos with a curriculum covering stellar dynamics, black holes, and the expansion of the universe. This specialization blends classical physics with modern astronomical techniques. Students will work with data from telescopes and simulations, preparing them for careers in research institutions, observatories, space agencies, and academic organizations.

B.Sc. Physics with Specialization in Nuclear Physics

Uncover the secrets of the atomic nucleus with a program that delves into nuclear reactions, radioactive decay, and the forces that govern subatomic particles. This specialization bridges the gap between classical physics and quantum theory, offering a deep dive into the energetic processes that power stars and fuel modern technologies. Students will engage with real-world applications through hands-on experiments, simulations, and radiation detection techniques, preparing them for dynamic careers in nuclear research labs, healthcare, energy sectors, and scientific institutions.
