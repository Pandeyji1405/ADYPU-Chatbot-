University Academic and Student Services Structure

This organizational structure outlines the academic and student support hierarchy within a university, beginning with executive leadership and extending to support services and mentorship roles.

1. Vice Chancellor

The Vice Chancellor stands at the top of the hierarchy, overseeing the university’s academic and administrative functions, providing strategic leadership, and ensuring institutional excellence.

2. Councillor

Reporting directly to the Vice Chancellor, the Councillor plays a vital advisory and liaison role, facilitating communication between administration, faculty, and students.

3. Student Council

Also reporting to the Vice Chancellor, the Student Council represents the student body and collaborates on initiatives related to student welfare, events, and feedback.

4. Dean of School

The Dean of School is responsible for managing the academic and administrative operations of a specific school within the university, ensuring quality education and smooth functioning.

5. Program Lead

Reporting to the Dean, the Program Lead oversees specific academic programs, ensuring curriculum delivery, faculty coordination, and academic standards.

6. Class Coordinator

Under the Program Lead, the Class Coordinator manages academic schedules, addresses student issues at the classroom level, and ensures communication between faculty and students.

At the base of the academic hierarchy, Mentors provide individual guidance and support to students in their academic, personal, and professional development.

8. Student Service Division

The Student Service Division operates parallel to the academic hierarchy, offering support services that address various student needs beyond the classroom.

Scholarship: Handles student financial aid, scholarship applications, and eligibility processing.

Documents: Manages academic records, certificates, and student documentation.

Accounts: Deals with tuition fees, financial transactions, and student account inquiries.

This integrated structure promotes both academic excellence and comprehensive student support, fostering a well-rounded educational environment.
