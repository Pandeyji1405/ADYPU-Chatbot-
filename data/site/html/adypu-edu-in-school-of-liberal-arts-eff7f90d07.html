Core features

The School of Liberal Arts embodies several core features that distinguish it as a unique and valuable institution within the university’s academic environment. These core features are central to the school’s identity and mission:

Interdisciplinary Approach

The School of Liberal Arts embraces an interdisciplinary approach to education. It encourages students to explore connections between various academic disciplines, fostering a holistic understanding of complex issues. This approach allows students to see the interrelatedness of knowledge and encourages creativity and innovation.

Diverse Curriculum

The school offers a diverse and comprehensive curriculum that encompasses a wide range of subjects, including literature, economics, psychology and International Studies. This breadth of knowledge equips students with a well-rounded education and prepares them for a variety of career paths.

Critical Thinking and Problem-Solving

Critical thinking is a cornerstone of the school's philosophy. Students are encouraged to question assumptions, analyze information, and evaluate arguments. This skill is developed through rigorous coursework and is essential for making informed decisions and solving real-world problems.

Ethical Awareness

The School of Liberal Arts promotes ethical awareness and moral reasoning. Students explore questions of ethics and values in their coursework, fostering a sense of social responsibility and ethical leadership.

Global Perspective

The school encourages students to develop a global perspective. Cultural competency and understanding diverse perspectives are vital in a globalized world, and students are prepared to engage with people from different backgrounds.

Experiential Learning

Experiential learning opportunities, such as internships, research projects, and community engagement, are integrated into the curriculum. These experiences provide students with real-world applications of their learning and help them connect theory to practice.

Placements

The School of Liberal Arts is committed to providing students with a well-rounded education that fosters intellectual growth and equips them with practical skills and experiences to succeed professionally. The placement program of the School of Liberal Arts is designed to connect students with a wide range of career opportunities, preparing them for diverse and rewarding career paths.

The School offers a diverse range of career pathways for its graduates. The interdisciplinary and holistic education provided equips students with a wide array of skills and knowledge, making them adaptable to various professions and industries. Here are some of the diverse career pathways that the School of Liberal Arts offers:

The School of Liberal Arts offers a diverse career pathways such as:

  • Journalism and Media: Opportunities in print, broadcast, and digital media.
  • Public Relations and Communications: Crafting and managing communication strategies for organizations.
  • Social Work and Counseling: Addressing societal and individual well-being.
  • Human Resources: Managing personnel and organizational development.
  • Education and Teaching: Nurturing the next generation as educators or educational administrators.
  • Research and Policy Analysis: Investigating and influencing social, economic, and political policies.
  • Nonprofit and NGO Management: Leading and managing organizations focused on social impact.
  • Creative Writing and Publishing: Exploring careers in writing, editing, and publishing.
  • Cultural Heritage Management: Preserving and promoting cultural artifacts and traditions.
  • International Relations and Diplomacy: Engaging in global affairs and diplomatic relations.
  • Event Management: Planning and executing events in various industries.
  • Entrepreneurship: Launching ventures aligned with liberal arts perspectives.
  • Law and Legal Services: Pursuing a career in law or legal support services.
  • Environmental Sustainability Advocacy: Promoting ecological awareness and sustainable practices.
  • Museum and Gallery Curatorship: Managing and curating cultural exhibits.

These diverse pathways reflect the interdisciplinary nature of a liberal arts education, providing students with a broad skill set applicable to various professional fields.
